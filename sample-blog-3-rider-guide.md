# Everything First-Time Riders Need to Know

Welcome to Going Platform! Whether you're replacing your daily commute, exploring a new city, or simply trying out ride-sharing for the first time, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know to have a smooth, safe, and enjoyable experience.

## Creating Your Account: Getting Started

The first step is downloading the Going app from your device's app store or visiting going.com from your browser. The signup process is straightforward and takes about 5 minutes.

You'll need:

- A valid email address or phone number
- A valid payment method (credit card, debit card, or digital wallet)
- Your legal name (must match payment method)
- A valid photo ID for verification purposes

During signup, Going requires identity verification for safety. You'll take a selfie and provide a photo of your ID. This process is fully automated and keeps both you and drivers safe. Verification typically completes within 24 hours, though most happen within minutes.

Once verified, you can request rides immediately. Pro tip: add multiple payment methods to your account in case one declines. It only takes 30 seconds and prevents booking frustration.

## Understanding Booking and Pricing

**How to request a ride:** Open the app, enter your destination, and tap "Request Ride." Going shows you an upfront fare estimate including base fare, distance, time, and any surge pricing. You see the total cost before confirming—no surprises at the end.

**What affects pricing:**

- **Base fare + distance**: Every ride starts with a base charge plus a per-mile rate. This varies by location but Going always shows estimates upfront.
- **Time of day (surge pricing)**: During high-demand periods, prices increase. Peak times (rush hour, weekend nights) typically see 1.5x to 3x price multipliers. You see surge pricing reflected in the quote before you confirm.
- **Route efficiency**: Longer routes cost more. Going calculates the fastest route using real-time traffic data, but if traffic changes mid-ride, time-based charges might increase slightly.
- **Vehicle type**: Going offers different service levels. Standard rides are most affordable; premium options (Going Premier, Going Plus) cost more but offer newer vehicles, premium drivers, and enhanced amenities.

**Money-saving tip:** Booking during off-peak hours (10am-3pm weekdays) typically offers the best rates. If your ride isn't time-sensitive, waiting 30 minutes can save $5-15. Also, shared rides (where Going pairs you with another passenger going similar directions) are typically 20-40% cheaper than solo rides.

## What to Expect on Your First Ride

**Before your driver arrives:**

Once you request a ride, you'll see your driver's name, vehicle details, photo, and real-time location. You can also see their rating (most Going drivers have 4.5+ ratings). Share your ride details with a trusted friend or family member using the in-app "Share Trip" feature—this is a great safety practice.

Some drivers arrive within 2-3 minutes; others take 10+ minutes depending on availability in your area. If a driver is taking longer than expected, you can cancel free of charge (you're only charged if you cancel after pickup).

**When your driver arrives:**

A brief introduction is normal. Many drivers will confirm your destination and offer conversation. Some drivers are chatty; others prefer quiet. Neither is wrong—adjust to their vibe. Always sit in the back seat for safety and comfort. If you have luggage, most drivers are happy to help load it.

**During the ride:**

Sit back and relax. Going drivers are trained professionals; they handle navigation and traffic. Many riders work, read, or check emails during rides. Some drivers offer amenities like phone chargers, water, or aux cable access to play your music. Taking advantage of these extras is fine—it's part of the Going experience.

If you notice something unusual (route seems wrong, temperature uncomfortable, etc.), politely mention it. Professional drivers appreciate feedback and will adjust. Safety is always the priority—if you ever feel unsafe, you can contact support immediately via the in-app emergency button.

**When you arrive:**

Thank your driver, grab any belongings, and exit safely. As you exit, you'll see a prompt to rate and tip your driver. Drivers appreciate tips (especially if they went above and beyond), but they're entirely optional and not required.

## The Rating and Review System

After each ride, you'll rate your driver on a 1-5 star scale. Ratings matter—they help the Going community identify the best drivers and protect passenger safety. Here's the rating guide:

- **5 stars**: Exceptional experience. Professional, friendly, safe, arrived early, or went out of their way.
- **4 stars**: Very good experience. Met expectations, was professional and safe.
- **3 stars**: Acceptable but unremarkable. Service met the basic standard.
- **2 stars**: Below expectations. Something was noticeably wrong (cleanliness, rudeness, unsafe driving).
- **1 star**: Serious issue. Safety concern, severe rudeness, major problem.

Only give 1-2 stars if there's a genuine issue. Most Going drivers are professionals working hard to earn their living. If you have a concern (unsafe driving, rude behavior, cleanliness), you can also report it directly to Going support with details.

Drivers also rate you. They rate based on respectfulness, punctuality, and condition of the vehicle afterward. Being a courteous passenger (don't be late, treat the car well, be respectful) ensures drivers give you good ratings, which can affect your booking priority.

## Managing Payments and Tipping

**Payment methods:** Your payment method is saved securely in the Going app. You'll be charged automatically after each ride—no cash exchange needed. You can update payment methods anytime in account settings.

**Tipping:** After each ride, the app prompts you to tip your driver. Tips are optional but appreciated. Typical tips are 15-20% of fare, though any amount helps. You can add tips through the app or in cash if you prefer. Unlike some platforms, Going doesn't require tipping—it's a genuine choice.

**Refunds and disputes:** If you see a charge you dispute, contact support within 30 days. Going investigates fare disputes and refunds fare errors. If you were charged for a ride you didn't complete, report it immediately.

## Safety: Your Top Priority

Going takes safety seriously. Here's what you should know:

**Before booking:**

- Check the driver's rating and vehicle details. If something seems off, request a different driver.
- Share your ride details with a trusted contact.
- Confirm the driver's name and vehicle match the app before entering.

**During the ride:**

- Sit in the back seat.
- Keep your phone accessible.
- Trust your instincts—if you feel unsafe, you can end the ride and request another driver.
- Never share personal information (home address, workplace, phone number) unless necessary.

**Going's safety features:**

- **Real-time trip tracking**: Your emergency contact can see your ride in real-time through the Share Trip feature.
- **Emergency button**: Use the in-app SOS button to contact authorities and Going support immediately.
- **Driver verification**: All Going drivers undergo background checks and vehicle inspections.
- **Recording**: Many Going drivers have in-car cameras (disclosed upfront) to protect both passengers and drivers.

Safety incidents are rare, but Go takes reports seriously. If you experience inappropriate behavior, contact support immediately with details and any identifying information.

## Using Special Features

**Scheduled rides:** Need a ride at a specific time? Book in advance. Scheduled rides for future trips (even just 30 minutes ahead) help Going ensure driver availability and often provide more stable pricing than surge-time requests.

**Shared rides:** Going's shared ride option pairs you with other passengers heading in similar directions. You'll make a brief detour, but fares are significantly cheaper. Shared rides are perfect for budget-conscious trips and less time-sensitive commutes.

**Accessibility features:** Going supports riders with disabilities. Note accessibility needs in your profile, and drivers trained in accessibility support will be prioritized. Communication with your driver about specific needs ensures a smooth experience.

**Ride history and receipts:** Every ride is documented in your app under "Trip History." You can view, download, or email receipts for expense reporting (great for business travel). This creates a perfect record.

## Common Questions Answered

**How much should I tip?**
Typical tips range from $1-5 for short rides or 15-20% for longer rides. But tip what you can afford—any amount is appreciated, and tipping is optional.

**What if I forget something in the car?**
Contact Going support within 24 hours. Include your trip details and what you forgot. Drivers often check lost and found immediately after rides. Going can connect you with your driver to arrange retrieval or return.

**Can I request a specific driver?**
Not directly, but your favorite drivers will recognize you if you use Going regularly. Many regular passengers develop preferred drivers who specifically accept their future rides.

**Is Going available at the airport?**
Most major airports have Going service. Check airport pickup zones (usually designated ride-share areas). Follow signs to the designated Going pickup spot. Avoid requesting pickups from inside the terminal—wait at the ride-share zone.

**How do I know the ride is safe?**
Check driver ratings, verify driver and vehicle details match your app, share your trip, and trust your instincts. Going drivers are verified professionals, but your comfort is priority one.

## Tips for the Best Experience

- **Book during off-peak hours** for better rates and shorter wait times.
- **Arrive at pickup spots early** so your driver isn't kept waiting.
- **Be respectful and courteous** with your driver. They're professionals working hard.
- **Rate honestly** and provide feedback that helps Going improve.
- **Report issues promptly** if something goes wrong. Support is responsive.
- **Save your favorite locations** (home, work, favorite restaurants) for faster booking.
- **Use the app's messaging feature** to communicate with your driver about specific pickups or dropoffs.
